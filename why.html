Here are the main advantages of joining the Barbers Federation of Ireland

A license signals training, skill, and safety knowledge to clients and employers. Makes it easier to build a loyal client base and get referrals.
Training covers cutting, shaving, skin and hair health, hygiene, and sanitation. You’re less likely to injure clients or spread infections, which protects your reputation.
Access to barbershops, salons, spas, hotels, cruise ships, and sometimes TV/film work. Some brands and chains only hire licensed professionals.
You can develop a niche: fades, beard work, hot towel shaves, textured hair, etc. Easier to move into teaching, shop ownership, or brand ambassador roles later.
Licenced and registered barbers can usually charge more or get better paying jobs. Shops often prefer or require licenced staff, which can mean a more stable income and tips.
In many places, once you’re licensed you can transfer or get recognition in other regions with less hassle.
Clients feel safer letting a licensed pro use razors and chemicals on their skin and hair. That trust turns into repeat visits and word-of-mouth marketing.
Many barbers become independent contractors or shop owners. With a license, it’s simpler to get insurance, permits, and sometimes small business loans.
Barbering is very social; you build a network of clients and other barbers who can help you find work, improve your skills, or collaborate.
